### Where I Started

- Business Intelligence Analyst at Goldman Sachs
    - Right out of college
    - Built dashboards and process automations for internal teams
    - Full-time job

### Transition to Side Projects

- Not an AI researcher or software developer, but a business analyst who understood data and operations
- Started small AI automation projects on the side
    - $500 here, $2,000 there
    - One-off builds for specific business needs
        - Workflow automated
        - Data moved between systems
        - Report generated automatically
- These unglamorous, defined tasks gave proof
    - Real projects

    - Real results
    - Each one built momentum

## From Projects to Partnerships

- **SECTION 2**: Small projects have a ceiling
    - Finish the work, get paid, relationship ends
    - Revenue resets to zero (e.g., from $4,375 to $0)
    - Next day: find a new client
- Figured this out quickly: shift from just building what client asked
    - Look at the broader business, dig deeper
        - Where is the company losing time?
        - Where else can AI create profit?
        - What unaddressed problems exist?
    - Uses same diagnostic skill from last lesson: identify right inputs and outputs

### From Broader Diagnostics to Bigger Engagements

- Apply same diagnostic skill (right inputs/outputs) proactively for the specific business
    - Don't wait for instructions — spot opportunities yourself
- Showing clients additional opportunities made engagements grow
    - 1 project → 2 → 3
    - Clients retained me by finding ongoing ways to improve operations

### Projects to Retainers

- Natural progression:
    - Client sees results → trusts judgment → wants ongoing work
- Shift from freelancer (task completion) to partner (business improvement)
    - Key prerequisite: build trust first through proven results

### Scaling Pricing with Value

- Pricing increased as results compounded
    - Early: $500 or $2,000 products
    - Retainers grew with delivered value
    - Agency minimum: $20K per month
- $20K/mo justified by lesson 1.2 math
    - Systems save/generate $100K/mo for business
    - $20K is fraction of value created
    - Clients pay because math works—not generosity
- High price point changes client math
    - Fewer clients needed for sustainability

### Achieving Seven-Figure Scale

- At $20K/mo pricing, client math shifts dramatically
        - Don't need hundreds of clients or massive agency
        - Small number of high-value partnerships adds up to seven figures
- Hit seven figures with agency and sold it—all before 24th birthday

### Conversion Funnel

- Not every project becomes a retainer
    - Key question: How many projects needed to land those retainers?

### Project-to-Retainer Conversion Funnel

- **\~25% conversion rate** is normal
    - 1 in 4 project clients upgrade to retainer
    - For 3 retainers needed: complete \~12 projects total
- Sounds like a lot, but map month by month — it's doable

**Month 1**

- Land 2-3 small projects
    - Learning
    - Building proof
    - Getting paid

**Month 2**

- Complete those projects + land 3-4 more
    - One early client upgrades: **first retainer!**

**Month 3**

- Running 3-4 new projects

**Month 3**

- 3-4 new projects running
    - First retainer paying monthly
    - Second client converts
    - Now: 2 retainers + project income

**Months 4-5**

- Third retainer comes in
    - Project income supplements retainers
    - At or near target income goal
- Happens in **months, not years**
