## You Don't Need to Be Technical. You Need to Think Clearly.

### The Misconception That Stops Most People

- Common worry: lacking skills despite hearing about **Automations**, **AI Agents**, **Workflows**, **APIs**, **Code**
    - Feels overwhelming if you've opened a tool and felt lost, or never written code
- Reality: Worry is based on misunderstanding of job requirements
    - Lesson shows exactly what you need (and don't need) to know
    - Answer is **simpler than you think** and changes view of the opportunity
- Most people assume AI work requires understanding how AI works at a **technical level** (e.g., how models work)
    - This stops most people from pursuing it

### SECTION 2: Inputs and Outputs

### Inputs and Outputs

- Every automation, workflow, or agent does **one thing**: takes inputs → processes → produces outputs
    - Your job: define inputs (what information goes in?) and outputs (what result needs to come out?)
- Middle part (actual building) handled by tools:

    1. You describe the problem
    2. Claude Code builds the solution
    3. You test, refine, deliver

- **This is not** a workaround or shortcut for people who can't code
    - This **is how the work actually gets done** now

### Who Succeeds in AI Work

- **Not the best coders** — they're the best at **defining what needs to happen** and **steering AI in the right direction**
    - If you can clearly describe **what goes in** and **what should come out**, you can build AI automations

### Inputs & Outputs in Practice

- Each of the **three profit levers** = **Inputs → Outputs**
    - Translates directly into practical examples
- **Example: Business wants more content** (social media dead for months, no time)
    - **Input**: Few bullet points about a recent client win or lesson from the founder
    - **Output**: LinkedIn post, email newsletter, or blog draft
        - All written in the company's voice

### Making Each Customer Worth More

- **Scenario**: Financial advisory firm gives personalized weekly reports to top clients only
    - Smaller clients get nothing because manual production is too expensive
- **Input**: Client portfolio data + recent market trends
- **Output**: Personalized weekly insights report for *each* client
    - Business couldn't afford this before, but now can offer (and charge) for it
    - Revenue per customer ↑ without acquiring new clients
- You don't need to build the system yourself
    - Describe the need → Claude Code builds it → ready to publish

### Cutting Costs

- **Scenario**: Company support team overwhelmed; half of daily tickets are routine questions (password resets, shipping statuses, return policies)
    - **Input**: Incoming support ticket
    - **Output**: Auto-responses to routine questions + flagged tickets for complex ones needing human
        - Support team handles **3.0x volume** without adding staff
        - Costs ↓ + capacity ↑
- **Key Pattern Across All Levers**: Value comes from knowing right inputs/outputs, **not** from understanding the tech
    - Describe need → Claude Code builds → test/refine/deliver

### The Real Skill Is Diagnosis

- **Core skill**: Understanding the business to spot AI opportunities
    - Value from knowing right **inputs/outputs**, not tech/engineering
    - **Clear thinking** about business needs
- **Hypotheticals** ✓ easy (like the 3 profit lever examples)
- **Real businesses**: Messy processes, unclear workflows, hidden problems (owner may not even see them)
    - **Hard part**: Figuring out actual inputs/outputs
    - **Requires**: Asking really good diagnostic questions

### Ask the Right Questions

- **1. Which process costs the most time?**
    - Start here to uncover high-impact opportunities
- **2. Where is manual work creating bottlenecks?**
    - Pinpoint repetitive manual tasks slowing down the business
- **3. What if 5 hours became 5 minutes?**
    - Identify tasks with massive time savings potential through automation
- **4. What adds no real value to the business?**
    - Spot employee activities that are pure overhead, ripe for AI elimination
- **Diagnosis as Business Skill**
    - Learned by asking questions, listening, understanding day-to-day operations
    - **Not** learned by studying code or technical skills
- **Who Succeeds as AI Partners**
    - Those who understand businesses deeply
    - Identify the **right problem** to solve
    - Describe it clearly for AI tools to build solutions

### Why the Opportunity is Even Bigger

- **Most People Are on the Sidelines**
    - Majority with business sense, communication skills, clear problem-thinking sit out
    - Convinced they need technical skills they lack
- **Real Talent Shortage**
    - Not just engineers
    - Shortage of people connecting AI to real business problems
    - Requires **business thinking**, not technical training
- **The Barrier Keeps Dropping**
    - Tools improve every month
    - Tasks needing developers 6 months ago now accessible to non-technical users
